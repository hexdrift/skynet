6. Postgres (bundled, single-replica)
   The bundled pgvector StatefulSet runs ONE pod backed by a {{ .Values.postgres.persistence.size }} PVC.
   Trade-offs to plan for before going to prod:

   - Node drains cause a brief outage. The PDB is OFF by default — a
     minAvailable=1 PDB on a single-replica StatefulSet would block every
     voluntary drain, which is usually worse than the ~30s eviction blip.
     Flip postgres.podDisruptionBudget.enabled=true if your operations
     prefer "refuse the drain" over "tolerate a blip".

   - PVC backups are your responsibility. Snapshot the
     {{ include "skynet.postgres.fullname" . }}-data-* PVC on a schedule
     (Velero, CSI VolumeSnapshot, or a cluster-wide backup operator).
     The chart does NOT take backups for you.

   - Vertical scale only. Bump postgres.resources.{requests,limits} when
     query load grows; horizontal scale is not supported by this chart.

   For real production loads, set externalDatabase.enabled=true and point
   at a managed pgvector cluster (Crunchy, CloudNativePG, RDS, etc.), then
   redeploy with postgres.enabled=false to remove the bundled StatefulSet.
{{- end }}
